Commitment to Child Safety

Every child has the right to feel safe

At ThriveAbility Care, child safety is at the heart of everything we do.

Our Commitment

Safe, supported, respected and empowered

At ThriveAbility Care, we place the safety and wellbeing of children at the centre of everything we do. We are committed to creating and maintaining environments where children feel safe, supported, respected and empowered.

We believe that every child has the right to grow up free from harm, abuse, neglect and exploitation. This includes children of all abilities, cultural backgrounds and life experiences. We are especially committed to the safety and inclusion of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander children, children with disability, children from culturally and linguistically diverse communities, and children who may be unable to live with their families.

Why It Matters

More than compliance — it’s a duty of care

Children deserve to feel safe and supported when accessing services. Child safety is more than just compliance, it is about protecting the rights, dignity and wellbeing of every child. By placing safety at the core of our practice, we build trust with children, families, carers and the community.

How We Keep Children Safe

Nine commitments we live by

Worker Screening

All staff and contractors must hold a valid Working with Children Check, NDIS Worker Screening Check, and current Police Check. No person will be engaged in any work with ThriveAbility Care without meeting those requirements.

Recruitment Practices

Our recruitment process includes child safety screening, reference checks and verification of qualifications to ensure only suitable people are engaged.

Training & Awareness

Staff receive mandatory training in child safety, mandatory reporting obligations, recognising signs of harm, trauma-informed care and culturally safe practices.

Code of Conduct

Our staff and contractors follow a clear Code of Conduct that sets expectations for safe, respectful and professional behaviour at all times.

Policies & Procedures

We have clear policies for responding to concerns, allegations and disclosures of child abuse or harm. All concerns are taken seriously, managed promptly and reported in line with legal and NDIS obligations.

Participation & Empowerment

We actively listen to children and involve them in decisions that affect them. Their views are respected and considered in planning supports.

Cultural Safety & Inclusion

We celebrate diversity and are committed to providing environments that are culturally safe and inclusive for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander children, children with disability and children from culturally and linguistically diverse communities.

Safe Environments

We ensure that all physical and online environments used by children are safe, monitored and free from risks of harm. This includes regular risk assessments of homes, community spaces and digital platforms to identify and address potential hazards.

Continuous Improvement

We regularly review our practices, seek feedback from children and families, and make improvements to strengthen our child safety systems.

Shared Responsibility

Everyone has a role

Child safety is a shared responsibility. Every member of our team from management to frontline staff, is responsible for creating and maintaining safe environments for children. Families and children are encouraged to speak up if they feel unsafe or have concerns.
